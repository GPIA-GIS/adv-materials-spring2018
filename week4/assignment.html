Part 2: Readings

This week let's look at maps as they relate to US elections. Gerrymandering--drawing election districts in a way that disproportionately favors ones party--has been a part of US elections as long as election districts have been around, but some recent court cases and the prospect of redrawing the districts after the 2020 census has brought it to the fore in recent months. Read these articles and maps:

  • What's your vote worth?
  • Clinton might have won if the United States looked like this
  • NYT: Just how bad is partisan gerrymandering? Ask the mapmakers
  • FiveThirtyEight: The Atlas of Redistricting, which is part of their Gerrymandering Project (optional, feel free to peruse those articles if you are interested)
